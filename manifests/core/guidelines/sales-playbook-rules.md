# Sales Playbook Rules

Applies to `generate.sales-playbook`, a five-section, rep-facing pre-call preparation document generated per contact.

## Offer framing

- Never state or imply the account already uses our product unless that is explicitly confirmed in the contact facts provided. Default to offer framing: "would it make sense to explore", "worth a look at", "curious whether".
- No confirmed usage signal in the facts means treat the account as a prospect, not a customer, in every section, not only the talk track.
- Never assume the contact has authority or ownership over the problem being proposed. Position as exploratory, not prescriptive.

## Why now: dated signals only

- The why now section may cite only signals that carry a date and fall within the recency window (the last 90 days). Undated or future-dated items are not signals, leave them out.
- If no qualifying signal exists in the contact facts, open on a relevant business priority for the account's stage or industry instead of stretching a stale or undated item into a trigger.

## The five sections

A playbook has exactly five sections, in this order:

1. **Account snapshot**: three concrete facts about the company, drawn from the contact facts provided. No invention.
2. **Why now**: one or two dated signals within the recency window that make this a good moment to reach out. Never undated or future-dated.
3. **Talk track**: three openers tied to the account facts. Offer framing throughout, never assume product ownership.
4. **Landmines**: what the rep should avoid saying, including any unconfirmed ownership claim.
5. **Next step**: one concrete, low-friction next action, not a menu of options.

## Length

The composed playbook, all five sections combined, must stay under 400 words total.

## No invention

Every concrete detail must trace back to the contact facts provided. If a fact is not in the record, do not write it. Use the section's fallback framing instead.

## Style

No em dashes. Use commas, periods, or a new sentence instead.
