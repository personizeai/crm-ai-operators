# Meeting Handoff

> When and how to hand a contact from agent → human (AE / SDR / CSM). The agent does discovery and qualification; the human takes the high-stakes conversation.

## Triggers — handoff happens when ANY of these fire

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| Reply classified as `Positive interest` (per `reply-handling.md`) | Immediate |
| Contact requests a meeting / demo / call | Immediate |
| Contact's `ai_score` crosses 80 with a strong signal in the last 7 days | Within 4h |
| Contact in tier-A account opens 3+ emails or visits pricing page | Within 1 business day |
| Reply contains pricing, contract, security, legal, or procurement language | Immediate |
| Reply contains a competitor's name + intent to evaluate | Within 4h |
| Multi-stakeholder engagement (2+ contacts at the same account engaging in the same week) | Within 1 business day |

## Handoff payload

The agent posts to the rep (Slack / CRM task / email — per repo config) with this structure:

```
Contact: [Name, Title, Company]
Trigger: <which trigger fired>
Account tier: <A/B/C>
ai_score: <0-100>
Account context: <2-sentence summary from companies.context>
Recent engagement: <last 3 conversations summarized in 1 line each>
Recommended angle: <1-sentence positioning, drawn from research + brand-voice>
Open issues: <any from workspace.open_issues>
```

The payload is generated by `act.notify-rep-handoff` and also written to `workspace.pending_tasks` for the rep to ack.

## What the agent does NOT do at handoff

- **Does not auto-send a meeting reply.** The rep crafts the response.
- **Does not auto-book on the rep's calendar** unless the rep has explicitly enabled auto-booking for this account.
- **Does not continue the sequence.** The active sequence pauses on `sequence_status = Replied` (or the trigger-equivalent). The rep decides whether to resume.
- **Does not loop in additional stakeholders** without rep approval.

## What the agent CONTINUES doing during/after handoff

- **Watches for new signals** on the account and surfaces them to the rep.
- **Logs the rep's actions** when observable (replies sent, meetings booked, deal-stage changes from CRM).
- **Drafts follow-ups** the rep can edit and send — clearly marked as drafts.
- **Re-qualifies** the account if the meeting outcome shifts the situation (e.g. won, churned, paused).

## Reverse handoff

When a rep hands a contact back to agent management (e.g. "nurture this for 6 months"):

- Rep sets `sequence_status = Paused` and adds a `notes` entry with the reason and reactivation date.
- Agent picks up management on or after the reactivation date.
- Re-qualification runs first; the contact may no longer match ICP after 6 months.

## Hard rules

- **Speed is the differentiator for positive interest.** Notify within 5 minutes of classification, not at the next batch.
- **Only one rep gets the handoff.** Round-robin or routed by account_owner — never both. Duplicate handoffs annoy reps and miscount metrics.
- **Handoff includes context, not just a notification.** A rep who has to dig through 12 emails to understand the deal context is a failure of the handoff, not a feature of "human in the loop".
- **The rep's response window matters.** If the rep doesn't act within 24h on a positive-interest reply, escalate to manager. Cold replies turn into closed deals only with speed.
